Can we make a responsible Internet of Things… in Shenzhen?

This documentary investigates how China's resources can be employed for the development of a responsible internet of things. In this documentary we follow a team of designers in their quest for a smart bike lock for their start-up Velocracy: a decentralised bike sharing service.

Shenzhen, labeled the Silicon Valley of Hardware, is the global capital of electronics manufacturing; 90% of hardware is made here. It is also home to some of the worlds largest bike sharing services with hundreds of thousands of bikes deployed in Shenzhen alone. This makes it the perfect environment to find a partner to supply the hardware for Velocracy.

But how suitable a place is Shenzhen for the development of responsible IoT products? The documentary shows how the Velocracy team tries to find the right partner to help them produce responsible this IoT product. Naturally, not all manufacturers and partners seemed equally facilitating towards the team's concerns. They become aware of the contrast between the modern design-oriented companies and the more traditional companies with backgrounds in hardcore manufacturing. Topics like open-source hardware were embraced by some and dismissed by others.
